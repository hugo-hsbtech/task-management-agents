# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-04-15*
*Context: Ezra Deal Flow WhatsApp Community & Referral Program*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** An exclusive WhatsApp community where Ezra shares deal opportunities in climate finance and referrers earn incentives for successful introductions.
**What it does:** Dan (Ezra's deal originator) posts curated deal opportunities — renewable energy, infrastructure, climate tech — to a WhatsApp Community announcement channel. Members (referrers) who know potential borrowers, brokers, or investors can express interest. Interested contacts get a private deal room (WhatsApp group with Dan + Mairi from customer success) where they receive an NDA and full deal details. Referrers earn a fee when a referred contact does business with Ezra.
**Product category:** Referral network / deal flow community (how members would describe it: "a deal flow group" or "a referral network for climate deals")
**Product type:** WhatsApp Community (free, no app to download, lives in members' existing WhatsApp)
**Business model:** Referral-fee based. No cost to join. Referrers earn incentives per the Ezra Referral Agreement. Fee structure depends on the type of introduction: borrower referrals earn perpetuity fees (on any deal the borrower signs, not just the first), investor referrals earn deal-specific fees (only if that investor signs that deal), and broker referrals may earn per-deal fees. Ezra monetises through the deals themselves.

## Target Audience
**Target members:** People who know people in climate finance, renewable energy, and infrastructure — specifically those connected to potential borrowers, but also brokers and investors. Likely UK/Europe-based professionals in finance, real estate, energy, or advisory roles.
**Decision-makers:** The referrers themselves. No corporate buying process — individuals decide to join.
**Primary use case:** "I know someone who might need climate finance. Ezra pays me if I introduce them and the deal closes."
**Jobs to be done:**
- Monetise my professional network without becoming a broker
- Get access to exclusive deal flow in climate/infrastructure
- Stay informed on what's happening in the market
**Use cases:**
- Referrer sees a deal pitch, knows a borrower who fits, DMs Dan, gets a private deal room
- Referrer passively stays in the community for market intel, refers someone months later
- Broker or investor joins to see deal flow and potentially co-invest or refer clients

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** People with strong professional networks in finance/energy have no easy, low-effort way to earn from introductions to climate finance opportunities.
**Why current solutions fall short:**
- Traditional referral networks require formal agreements per-deal and heavy admin
- Word-of-mouth referrals are informal — no tracking, no guaranteed payout
- Climate finance is niche — generic referral platforms don't cover it
- Most deal flow is hidden behind closed doors (investment banks, PE firms)
**What it costs them:** Missed referral income. They already know the right people but have no mechanism to act on it.
**Emotional tension:** "I could have made money from that introduction but I didn't know who to send them to" / "I hear about deals too late"

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** No direct competitor doing WhatsApp-based referral communities for climate finance specifically. YWRC (You Will Raise Capital) is the closest model — an investment strategist community using the same WhatsApp Community structure — but focused on M&A/business valuations, not climate.
**Secondary:** LinkedIn deal-sharing groups, broker networks, industry WhatsApp groups (informal, unstructured, no incentive)
**Indirect:** Traditional referral agreements (one-off, high-friction, no community element)
**How each falls short:** No community feel, no ongoing deal flow, no structured incentive, no private deal rooms, no dedicated customer success follow-up.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- Curated climate-specific deal flow (not generic finance)
- Structured referral incentive with a formal agreement
- Private deal rooms with NDA process (professional, not casual)
- Dan's personal involvement (trust, expertise, relationships)
- Mairi's customer success follow-up (no lead goes cold)
- Zero friction to join (it's WhatsApp, already on your phone)
**How we do it differently:** Community-first approach. Members get value (market intel, deal flow) even if they never refer anyone. The referral incentive is a bonus on top of genuine community membership.
**Why that's better:** Low commitment, high trust. No app to download, no login to remember, no formal onboarding process. Just join and start seeing deals.
**Why members stay:** Ongoing value from market insights + the optionality of earning referral fees at any time.

## Objections & Anti-Personas
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "I don't know anyone who needs climate finance" | That's OK — you'll learn what we look for. Many referrers didn't know they had relevant contacts until they saw a specific deal. |
| "Is this legitimate / will I actually get paid?" | We have a formal Referral Agreement. [Name] earned GBP X from their last referral. (Social proof) |
| "I don't want to spam my contacts" | You're not spamming anyone. When you see a deal that fits someone you know, you just introduce us. We handle the rest privately. |

**Anti-persona:** People looking for investment advice or personal finance help. This is B2B deal flow, not retail investing. Also: anyone who expects passive income without actually making introductions.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** "I keep hearing about climate finance deals after they close. I'm always late." / "I know people who need this but I don't know who to send them to."
**Pull:** "I could earn referral fees from my existing network, with zero upfront cost, on a platform I already use."
**Habit:** Existing informal referral arrangements (calling a friend at a bank, forwarding an email). Low-structure, low-accountability.
**Anxiety:** "Will this be spammy?" / "Will my contacts trust this?" / "Is Ezra legitimate?" / "What if the deal doesn't close — do I still get paid?"

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| Referrer | A community member who introduces contacts to Ezra |
| Borrower referral | Referrer introduces a borrower — perpetuity fee (earns on any deal the borrower signs) |

| Deal pitch | The announcement Dan posts to the community about a new opportunity |

## Brand Voice
**Tone:** Professional but approachable. Confident, not salesy. Dan speaks like a trusted colleague sharing an opportunity, not a marketer pushing a product.
**Style:** Direct, concise, conversational. WhatsApp-native — short paragraphs, emoji bullets where appropriate, no corporate jargon. Voice notes encouraged for personal touch.
**Personality:** Trustworthy, knowledgeable, exclusive (but not elitist), action-oriented, human
